Impermanent loss is an important concept within liquidity pools.

This is because there is a difference in potential profit or loss between keeping your coins in a pool and keeping them in your wallet.

This concept comes to life when you withdraw from a pool based on the price difference between the coins within the pool and their external market value.

It is a complex function that is affected by various factors such as the timing of price change between two coins and the rate of price change.

This will help you determine whether the potential return from fees + compensation outweighs the risk of impermanent loss.

Truly understanding impermanent loss requires understanding complex concepts and variables that cannot be fully captured in simplified explanations.

Let me explain with a very simple example.

approximately As of this writing, 1 XLM is equal to 22 SSLX (values of $0.12 and $0.0054 respectively).

Initial investment: $24

100 XLM + 2200 SSLX = $12 + $12 = $24

Assume that the value of XLM doubles while SSLX remains constant.

The value of XLM has doubled to $0.24, while SSLX remains at $0.0054.

Only XLM increased by 100%, so now XLM is 100*0.24=24$. SSLX price remains the same at $12, so it will be adjusted to $36.

So in the liquidity pool, they are adjusted at a nearly 1:1 ratio, at about $18 each.

As a result, in this hypothetical example, the value of the pool increases from $24 to $36 after the price change.

Pool re-evaluation after XLM price increase:

$18=75 XLM * $0.24

$18=3311 SSLX * $0.0054

The new value of the pool after the price change is $36.

However, if the initial 200 XLM had not been placed in the liquidity pool, the $24 invested in XLM alone would have doubled to $48.

So the impermanent loss makes a difference of $12.

To summarize here (assuming SSLX values remain constant):

The XLM value remains constant and the SSLX value remains constant. - XLM quantity will remain the same and SSLX quantity will remain the same.

The XLM value increases and the SSLX value remains constant. - XLM quantity decreases and SSLX quantity increases.

The XLM value decreases and the SSLX value remains constant. - XLM quantity increases and SSLX quantity decreases.

If the value of SSLX, which has remained constant, begins to decline, It may fall further from $36, resulting in additional losses.

It is important to note that impermanent losses will occur if the percentage of tokens in the liquidity pool changes due to price fluctuations.

The greater the price difference between tokens, the greater the impermanent loss.

This is a risk that must be considered when providing liquidity to such pools.
